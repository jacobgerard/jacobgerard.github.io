Over this next month, I’ll be summarizing all the interesting things I’ve been studying and working on in my free time this past year. It was a packed schedule for all aspects of my life, and taking time at the end of the day to sit down and learn something new wasn’t always the top priority. But I managed to squeeze in enough to have a few posts I’d like to share! The basic outline is:

  • Books I’ve Read
  • Coding Projects/Skills
  • Finance Research

Books

I’ve read 12 books so far (looking to add at least 1 more to the tally) and wrote up 3 posts with notes and quotes! I’ll link those here and then post my impressions of a few of the others that I learned a lot from. I highly recommend The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel as I think everybody can get a lot out of it. Plus its a fairly quick read.

  • Napoleon: A Life - Andrew Roberts
  • Ender’s Game - Orson Scott Card
  • Genghis Khan and the Making of the Modern World - Jack Weatherford
  • Where the Crawdads Sing - Delia Owens
  • Dune - Frank Herbert
  • Verity - Colleen Hoover
  • China: A History - John Keay
  • Starship Troopers - Robert Heinlein
  • Psychology of Money - Morgan Housel
    • Good stories about how to be more successful in personal finances by staying the course and recognizing that some people or industries just get lucky. Long term investing is the surest path to success and you need to save just for unexpected life events, not necessarily only for big purchases.
  • Catch-22 - Joseph Heller
  • Outliers: The Story of Success - Malcolm Gladwell
    • Success is tough to achieve just by hard work alone. Most of the extremely successful people in the world had very unusual circumstances that put them in positions to be the best at something right when it was necessary. There is not much to do to control that. But it also points out that the people you surround yourself with are very likely to have a large impact on your standing in the world.
  • A Brief History of Time - Stephen Hawking
    • Stunning overview of very complicated math and science in an easily digestable format. This is an inspiration to start looking into those tough problems with a new lens about how they all fit together into grand theories of the universe.

Skills

Coding and cybersecurity study time has been hard to come by. I mainly took the small technologies I was interested in and found a way to work them into my at-home, personal finance website. This is a simple site that I host on my desktop and is only accessible within my home network. I originally wrote the business logic in Python and kept all data tracked across a few CSV files. Last year I leveled up to a basic website running on Flask and a SQLite database.

In the past 12 months, I’ve learned a lot more efficient ways to manage and present the data. During the spring I optimized the backend with more useful queries. Summer was my time to actually sit down and learn good design practices in CSS and the basics of Javascript. I then turned a few queries into single page applications. Finally, during the last few months I was able to cover some basic Data Science and plotting with matplotlib. Along the way I also worked on my VIM and LATEX skills as both of these seem incredibly useful and I enjoy working in them.
